# DOH Document Tracking System — User Manual

This guide is for coordinators, staff, and administrators who use the web application day to day. It describes screens and tasks in plain language. Your organization’s IT or admin team can give you the exact website address (URL) and account rules for your office.

---

## 1. What this system does

The DOH Document Tracking System helps you:

- Register and track documents (with a document control number and routing information).
- Send documents forward (routing) to other offices and action officers.
- See items addressed to you (Inbox) and items you are working on (Outbox).
- Run reports (by subject, office, dates, overdue items, and more).
- Keep an audit trail of important actions (who did what and when), where your role allows.

The app runs in a web browser. You sign in with your employee code and password.

## 3. Signing in and signing out

### Sign in

1. Open the system URL in your browser.
2. Enter your Employee code and Password.
3. Click to sign in.

If you see a message that your account is pending approval, an administrator or an authorized office head must verify your account before you can use the system (see [Section 4.2](#42-account-verification)).

### Sign out

Use Logout in the sidebar when you finish, especially on a shared computer.

### Profile (your own details)

Use your profile control in the header to update name, employee code, office, user level, and other allowed fields. Some fields may be restricted depending on your role.

---

## 4. Registering and account approval

### 4.1 Self-registration

If registration is enabled on your site:

1. Open the Register page (link from the login screen).
2. Fill in required fields (e.g. name, employee code, office, user level, password).
3. Submit the form.

### 4.2 Account verification

- New accounts may need verification before login works fully.
- Administrators can verify users from Registered Users.
- Action Officers who are Office Representative = Yes for an office can verify pending users in that same office.

Action Officers who are not administrators typically only see users from their own office on the Registered Users list.

---

## 5. Main navigation (sidebar)

After you sign in, the left sidebar gives access to the main areas. What you see depends on your user level (role).

| Area | Typical use |
|------|----------------|
| Home | Shortcuts and summary information. |
| Outbox | Documents you create or hold; add routing, attachments, and details. |
| Inbox | Documents routed to you or where you are involved as listed. |
| Reference Tables | Master lists (offices, document types, action required, etc.). Usually maintained by trained staff or admins. |
| Reports | Various listings and the Audit Trail. |
| Trash | Soft-deleted documents (restore or permanent delete, depending on role). |
| Registered Users | User accounts (not shown to End-User role; shown to other roles per policy). |

You can expand or collapse the sidebar (including on smaller screens) using the controls provided.

Dark / light theme is available from the header so you can choose a comfortable view.

---

## 6. Home

Home gives you quick access to common destinations and may show counts or recent activity (depending on setup and your role).

Use it as a starting point to open Outbox, Inbox, Reports, or Trash.

---

## 7. Outbox (document source)

Outbox is where document records live: subject, type, control number, routing, attachments, and remarks.

### 7.1 Who sees what

- End-Users usually see only their own documents.
- Action Officers (and similar roles) typically see documents relevant to their office (and related rules set by your organization).
- Administrators may see all documents.

If something is missing, check with your coordinator: your office on your profile must match how documents are recorded.

### 7.2 Common tasks

- Add a new document using the add / create control.
- Open a document to view or edit details (subject, type, offices, employees, etc.).
- Attach files where the system allows uploads.
- Add routing rows (destinations, action officer, dates, action required) so the document can move through the workflow.
- Print or export using buttons on the page, if available.
- Move to Trash when a document should be removed from the active list (it may still be recoverable from Trash for a limited time).

### 7.3 Routing slip

Some workflows open a routing slip view suitable for printing. Follow your office procedure for when to print or save a PDF.

---

## 8. Inbox

Inbox lists documents that are routed to you or where you appear as the action officer (or related rules your office uses).

- Use search or filters on the page if available.
- Open a row to receive, update, or act on a document according to your process.

---

## 9. Trash

Trash holds documents that were soft-deleted from Outbox.

- You may restore a document back to Outbox or permanently delete it, depending on your role and local policy.
- Items may be removed automatically after a defined number of days—your coordinator can confirm the retention rule.

---

## 10. Reference tables

Reference tables define dropdown values used across the system, for example:

| Table | Purpose (summary) |
|-------|---------------------|
| Office | Office names used on documents and profiles. |
| Region | Regional reference data. |
| Document Type | Types of documents. |
| Action Required | Standard action labels for routing. |
| Action Taken | Standard labels when an action is completed. |
| Action Officer | Reference list aligned with Action Officer roles. |
| Document / Action Required Days | Timing reference for required actions. |
| User Levels | Labels for access levels (managed carefully). |

Only staff who are trained for this should add, edit, or delete rows. Wrong entries can affect reports and routing.

---

## 11. Reports

Under Reports, you may have access to listings such as:

| Report | Helps you… |
|--------|------------|
| By Releasing Officer | Find documents by releasing officer. |
| By Date and Time | Filter by time period. |
| By Control No. | Look up a specific control number. |
| By Subject | Search by subject text. |
| By Office | Group or filter by office. |
| By Office Control No. | Use office-specific numbering if used. |
| By Action Officer | Focus on a given action officer. |
| Overdue Report | See routing steps past their required date/time. |
| Audit Trail | Review system-logged events (who/what/when), within your access rights. |

Most report pages let you search, paginate, and often print or export (e.g. Excel or Word) for meetings and files.

### Audit Trail — date filter

On Audit Trail, you can often set a date range (from / to) so you only see events in that period. Set the same date twice if you need a single day. Clear the dates to load the default window (your admin can explain limits).

---

## 12. Registered Users (non–End-Users)

If your role allows Registered Users:

- Administrators can usually add, edit, verify, and remove users (within policy).
- Action Officers may see only users in their office and can approve pending users when they are Office Representative = Yes for that office.

Always follow data privacy and HR rules for your organization when handling account data.

---

## 13. Roles in brief

| Role | Typical access |
|------|----------------|
| End-User | Own documents in Outbox; Inbox as routed; Trash for own deleted items; Home and reports as configured. No Registered Users. |
| Action Officer | Office-scoped (or broader) document lists; reference tables and reports as assigned; Registered Users limited to same office for listing/approval where applicable. |
| Administrator | Broad access: all users, full document lists (unless restricted by policy), and maintenance of reference data. |

Exact permissions are set by your organization and user level names in the system.

---

## 14. Tips for coordinators

1. Train users on Outbox → routing and Inbox → receive/act using one or two real examples.
2. Keep office names and user profiles consistent so reports and office-based filters stay accurate.
3. Use Overdue Report and Audit Trail for follow-ups and quality checks.
4. Document your local URLs, who approves accounts, and retention for Trash on a shared internal wiki or memo—not only inside this app.

---

## 15. Getting help

- Login or password: follow your office procedure (often the IT helpdesk or system administrator).
- Wrong data or missing documents: confirm employee code, office, and verification status with your coordinator.
- Bugs or errors: note the page, time, and what you clicked; contact technical support with a screenshot if allowed.
